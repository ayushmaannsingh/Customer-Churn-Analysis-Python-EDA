# 📊 Customer Churn Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

Customer churn is one of the most important business metrics for subscription-based companies. This project analyzes customer data to identify the factors that contribute to customer churn and provides insights that can help businesses improve customer retention.

Using Python and data visualization techniques, the project explores customer demographics, account information, service usage patterns, and contract details to understand why customers leave.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze customer churn patterns.
* Identify key factors influencing customer attrition.
* Perform Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).
* Generate actionable business insights.
* Visualize customer behavior and churn trends.

## 🔍 Key Findings

### 1. Churn Rate

* Approximately 26.5% of customers have churned.
* Most customers remain with the company, but a significant portion leaves.

### 2. Contract Type Impact

* Customers with Month-to-Month contracts show the highest churn rate.
* Long-term contracts have better retention.

### 3. Senior Citizens

* Senior citizens exhibit a relatively higher churn rate than non-senior customers.

### 4. Customer Tenure

* Customers with shorter tenure are more likely to churn.
* Loyalty increases with longer tenure periods.

### 5. Additional Services

Customers without:

* Online Security
* Online Backup
* Device Protection
* Tech Support

show a higher probability of churning.

### 6. Payment Method

* Customers using Electronic Check have a higher churn rate compared to other payment methods.

---

## 📈 Business Recommendations

* Offer retention incentives for Month-to-Month customers.
* Promote long-term contract plans.
* Improve customer support services.
* Encourage adoption of value-added services.
* Create targeted retention campaigns for high-risk customers.
